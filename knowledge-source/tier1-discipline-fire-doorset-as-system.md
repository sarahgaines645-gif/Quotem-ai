# Discipline — Fire Doorset Is a Certified Assembly, Not Component Codes

> **Rule:** A fire doorset is a single certified assembly (leaf + frame + hinges + closer + seals + intumescent + letterplate + lock). Substituting any component for one not on the certificate's data sheet voids the FD30 / FD60 rating.
> **Audience:** specifiers, surveyors, contractors/installers, building-control officers, landlords commissioning the work, fire-risk assessors.
> **Last verified:** 2026-04-26

## What "doorset" means

A **doorset** is the complete factory-tested assembly: leaf, frame, hinges, closer, intumescent + cold-smoke seals, letterplate (if any), viewer (if any), lock/latch, threshold. It is tested as a unit to **BS EN 1634-1** (fire-resistance test for door, shutter and openable window assemblies).
Source: https://knowledge.bsigroup.com/products/fire-resistance-tests-for-doors-shutters-and-openable-windows-and-elements-of-building-hardware-part-1-fire-resistance-tests-for-doors-shutters-and-openable-windows

A "door on a frame" assembled from separately bought components is **not** a tested doorset and cannot carry the FD30/FD60 mark unless every component matches the original certification data sheet.

## Why mixing components changes the rating

Fire performance depends on the interaction of leaf, seals, hinges, closer and frame under heat. A test certificate covers the **specific configuration tested under BS EN 1634-1**. Substituting a hinge from a different manufacturer, or fitting a non-listed letterplate, takes the assembly outside the tested configuration and the certificate no longer covers it.

The code of practice for installation is **BS 8214:2016** — Timber-based fire door assemblies, code of practice. It sets out gap tolerances, intumescent positioning, hinge fixing depths and other site requirements; departing from these breaks the test evidence in the same way as a component swap.
Source (BS 8214): https://knowledge.bsigroup.com/products/timber-based-fire-door-assemblies-code-of-practice

The BWF Fire Door Alliance publishes installer-facing guidance on this principle — see https://firedoors.bwf.org.uk/ — but the underlying rule is the BS EN 1634-1 test report itself.

## Component standards (each must match the doorset's data sheet)

- **Leaf + frame fire test:** BS EN 1634-1 (current test reference; older BS 476-22 evidence still exists on legacy doors).
  Source: https://knowledge.bsigroup.com/products/fire-resistance-tests-for-doors-shutters-and-openable-windows-and-elements-of-building-hardware-part-1-fire-resistance-tests-for-doors-shutters-and-openable-windows
- **Code of practice for fire door assemblies:** BS 8214:2016.
  Source: https://knowledge.bsigroup.com/products/timber-based-fire-door-assemblies-code-of-practice
- **Hinges:** BS EN 1935. Grade 13 (200,000 cycles, doors up to 120 kg) is the common spec for fire doors; Grade 14 (200,000 cycles, up to 160 kg) is the highest. Three per leaf, ball-bearing, with intumescent pads.
  Source: https://knowledge.bsigroup.com/products/building-hardware-single-axis-hinges-requirements-and-test-methods
- **Closers:** BS EN 1154. The fire/smoke digit (position 4 in the eight-digit code) must be **1** for fire doors.
  Source: https://knowledge.bsigroup.com/products/building-hardware-controlled-door-closing-devices-requirements-and-test-methods
- **Locks/latches:** BS EN 12209, UKCA/CE marked, tested for fire-rated use.
  Source: https://knowledge.bsigroup.com/products/building-hardware-locks-and-latches-mechanically-operated-locks-latches-and-locking-plates-requirements-and-test-methods
- **Intumescent + cold-smoke seal:** combined fire+smoke seals are required for FD30S / FD60S (smoke-rated). Section size and positioning follow the doorset data sheet.
- **Fire-rated letterplate:** intumescent-lined, listed on the doorset's data sheet. A non-listed letterplate is one of the most common cause of voided ratings.

## UK certification schemes

- **Certifire** (Warringtonfire) — third-party certification of doorsets and components. https://www.warringtonfire.com/product-certification/certifire
- **Q-Mark Fire Door Scheme** (BM TRADA) — third-party certification scheme for manufacture and installation. https://bmtrada.com/services/q-mark
- **BWF Fire Door Alliance** — industry scheme covering manufactured doorsets. https://firedoors.bwf.org.uk/

A compliant FD30 flat-entrance doorset carries a permanent label or plug identifying the certification body, scheme and unique reference traceable to the test report.

## Statutory hooks

- **Approved Document B Volume 1** — flat-entrance doors from common parts to flats must achieve at least FD30S with self-closing.
  Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/fire-safety-approved-document-b
- **Fire Safety (England) Regulations 2022** — Responsible Person duties: quarterly checks of common-parts fire doors; annual checks of flat-entrance fire doors in buildings >11m.
  Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/fire-safety-england-regulations-2022-fire-door-guidance/fire-safety-england-regulations-2022-fire-door-guidance
- **Building Safety Act 2022** — for higher-risk buildings (>18m or 7+ storeys, 2+ residential units), fire-door work falls under the gateway / Accountable Person regime.
  Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2022/30/contents/enacted

## In practice

Treat the fire doorset as one certified line item:

- **Right:** one assembly, supplied and installed to the manufacturer's data sheet, with the certification label intact and visible.
- **Wrong:** door + frame + hinges + closer + letterplate sourced separately and bolted together on site. The result is not an FD30 doorset, and the Responsible Person, installer and (where applicable) Accountable Person are exposed under the regulations above.

## Common errors and red flags

- A "fire door" specified or supplied without naming the certified doorset (manufacturer + model + data sheet reference).
- An FD30 claim made on a site-assembled door with no third-party label (Certifire / Q-Mark / BWF Fire Door Alliance).
- A non-fire letterplate or non-listed hinge fitted to a fire doorset — the certificate no longer applies.
- Gap tolerances, threshold seals or intumescent positioning that depart from BS 8214 / the data sheet.
- For FD60 or any work on a building >18m, the work falls under the Building Safety Act gateway regime — competent fire-door installer (Q-Mark Installer or equivalent) and Accountable Person involvement is mandatory.
