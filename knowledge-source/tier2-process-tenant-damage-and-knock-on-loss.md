# Process — Tenant damages property; another tenant suffers knock-on loss

> **Trigger:** Tenant 1 damages a structural / security element (door, lock, window). The damage compromises safety and another occupant (tenant 2 / housemate / lawful visitor) suffers knock-on harm — burglary, weather damage, injury.
> **Audience:** Landlord, letting agent, property manager, surveyor, contractor receiving the emergency call.
> **Last verified:** 2026-04-28
> **Note:** Legal duties below are cited to primary source. Step-by-step sequence is best-practice landlord process — not statutory in itself, but built around the statutory clocks.

---

## Day 0 — make safe (within 24 hours)

Make-safe is a **duty**, not a courtesy. The landlord must restore basic security regardless of who caused the damage. Recovery from the responsible tenant happens later, through the deposit / county court — not by leaving the property unsafe.

Acceptable make-safe in the first 24h:
- Board-up service for a destroyed door / window
- Temporary lock change by a locksmith
- Replacement of glass with security board if the original cannot be sourced same-day

**Do not** wait for the "regular contractor" if they can't attend within 24h. Use any competent provider and document the cost — recovery comes later.

## Legal duties triggered

| Duty | Source | What it requires |
|---|---|---|
| Repair of structure & exterior — including external doors | **s11 Landlord and Tenant Act 1985** ([legislation.gov.uk](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1985/70/section/11)) | Landlord must keep in repair the structure and exterior of the dwelling. External doors are structure for s11 purposes (long-standing case law). |
| Fitness for human habitation | **s9A & s10 Landlord and Tenant Act 1985** (added by Homes (Fitness for Human Habitation) Act 2018) ([legislation.gov.uk](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1985/70/section/9A)) | Implied covenant — applies to any tenancy granted or renewed after 20 March 2019. A dwelling without a securable external door is not fit for habitation. |
| Duty of care to occupiers and visitors | **s4 Defective Premises Act 1972** ([legislation.gov.uk](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1972/35/section/4)) | "Where premises are let under a tenancy which puts on the landlord an obligation to the tenant for the maintenance or repair of the premises, the landlord owes to all persons who might reasonably be affected by defects in the state of the premises a duty to take such care as is reasonable in all the circumstances..." This is the route by which tenant 2's loss can become the landlord's exposure if the landlord delayed unreasonably. |
| Awaab's Law — emergency hazard timeframes (social tenancies only) | **Hazards in Social Housing (Prescribed Requirements) (England) Regulations 2025** — see `tier2-awaabs-law.md` | 24h to investigate and make safe, 3 working days for written summary, 5 working days for permanent repair after investigation. Phase 1 from 27 October 2025. |
| HHSRS Hazard 12 — entry by intruders | **Housing Health and Safety Rating System (England) Regulations 2005** — see `tier2-hhsrs-pointer.md` | A non-securing external door is HHSRS Hazard 12. Local authority EHO can serve an Improvement Notice (Cat 1) or Hazard Awareness Notice (Cat 2). |

## Who pays — the liability map

| Cost | Who pays initially | Recoverable from tenant 1? |
|---|---|---|
| Emergency make-safe (board-up, temp lock) | **Landlord** — flows from s11 / s9A duty | **Yes**, if breach of tenancy clause + evidence of fault. |
| Permanent repair (new door / frame / locks) | **Landlord** | **Yes**, same conditions. |
| Tenant 2's stolen contents | **Tenant 2's contents insurance** (primary). If uninsured, tenant 2 may have a claim against tenant 1 (criminal/civil) or against the landlord under **Defective Premises Act 1972 s4** if landlord delayed unreasonably. | N/A — landlord recovery is separate from tenant-to-tenant claim. |
| Tenant 2's alternative accommodation if uninhabitable | Depends on cause + landlord insurance (rent-loss / alternative-accommodation cover) | Same as above. |

**Recovery routes from tenant 1 (in order):**

1. **Pay-on-invoice** — issue itemised invoice with photo evidence; allow reasonable time (commonly 14–28 days).
2. **Deposit deduction** at end of tenancy — submit photos + invoice + tenancy clause to the deposit scheme (DPS / TDS / MyDeposits).
3. **Scheme adjudication** if tenant 1 disputes — free, decision typically within ~28 days. Adjudicators want all three of: clause, evidence, invoice.
4. **County Court small claims** (Money Claim Online) if loss exceeds the deposit and the deduction is refused / rejected. Current small claims track limit is **£10,000** for personal injury / damages claims. ([gov.uk fees & process](https://www.gov.uk/make-court-claim-for-money))

## Step-by-step (the sequence that protects the landlord)

### Hours 0–24

1. **Acknowledge the report in writing** within 1 hour. Text or email is fine; the timestamp matters more than the medium.
2. **Photo evidence — before any work.** Wide shot, close-up of damage, close-up of forced-entry / tool marks, timestamp visible.
3. **Make safe.** Board-up / temp lock / lock change — whichever is fastest. Get the contractor's invoice with date and time on it.
4. **Police report** if a crime occurred (criminal damage by tenant 1 / theft from tenant 2). Call **101** for non-emergency or **999** if in progress. Get the **Crime Reference Number** — insurers and adjudicators ask for it.
5. **Awaab's Law summary letter** if social tenancy — within 3 working days, written summary of findings to the tenant who reported. Required by the 2025 Regulations.

### Days 1–7

6. **Identify cause** — was it forced (tenancy breach), accidental (still tenant-responsible under most ASTs), or wear-and-tear (landlord, no recovery)?
7. **Notify the landlord insurer.** Many policies require notification within 24h or 48h; check schedule. Even if you won't claim, the notification protects future cover.
8. **Permanent repair quote** — replace door + frame + locks to original spec or better. Reference `tier1-quotem-default-specs.md` for the standard external door spec.
9. **Permanent repair completed.** For social tenancies, within 5 working days of investigation (Awaab's). For PRS, no statutory deadline — but Defective Premises Act exposure grows daily, especially if any further harm to tenant 2 follows.
10. **Itemised invoice to tenant 1** with photos, receipts, tenancy clause reference. Allow reasonable time to pay.

### End of tenancy

11. **Deposit deduction** for any unpaid balance — submit to deposit scheme with full evidence file.
12. **If disputed** — scheme adjudicates. If rejected and loss exceeds the deposit, **small claims court** is the route.

## Evidence file — what to keep

- Photos: wide / close-up / timestamped (before make-safe, after make-safe, after permanent repair)
- Make-safe invoice with date and time
- Permanent repair invoice
- Tenancy agreement clause covering tenant-caused damage
- Written admission from tenant 1 OR statement from tenant 2 / witness OR CCTV
- Police Crime Reference Number (if applicable)
- All tenant correspondence (text / email / letter)
- Insurance claim reference (if applicable)
- For social tenancies: the Awaab's Law written summary of findings + dates

## When to escalate — and to whom

| Trigger | Escalate to |
|---|---|
| Criminal damage / theft | Police (101 or 999) — for the Crime Reference Number |
| Structural damage exceeding excess | Insurer — within 24–48h |
| Tenant 1 disputes liability AND loss exceeds deposit AND exceeds £100 | Solicitor / county court small claims |
| Property is HMO or sub-let and damage may have triggered licence breach | Council EHO / housing standards team |
| Deduction disputed at end of tenancy | Deposit scheme adjudicator (free) |
| Tenant 2 uninsured and considering claim against landlord | Tell tenant 2 to seek **independent** legal advice — never advise them on their own claim against you |

## Common mistakes that cost landlords money

1. **Repairing first, photographing later.** Destroys the evidence chain. Adjudicators reject deduction claims with no "before" photos.
2. **Waiting for the regular contractor.** Make-safe is a Day-0 duty. Any competent provider counts.
3. **Trying to deduct from rent rather than deposit.** Landlord cannot unilaterally reduce rent. Keep rent and damage strictly separate.
4. **No written acknowledgment to the reporting tenant.** Breaks the audit trail and (for Defective Premises Act purposes) the timeline of "when the landlord knew".
5. **Telling tenant 2 "you should've had insurance."** That's not a defence to a Defective Premises Act claim. The landlord may still be liable for delay.
6. **Assuming a tenancy clause is enough on its own.** Adjudicators want clause + photos + invoice + timeline. Missing any one and the deduction is often refused.
7. **Forgetting Awaab's Law for social tenancies.** The 24h make-safe / 3-day summary / 5-day repair clock is statutory, not advisory.
8. **Charging tenant 1 for "betterment".** Replacement must be like-for-like. If the broken door was 8 years old, you cannot invoice for a brand-new equivalent — depreciation must be applied. Adjudicators routinely reduce claims for betterment.

## What QB2 should never do

- **Never quote a definitive liability split.** Outcomes depend on tenancy wording, evidence quality, and adjudicator / court discretion. Always recommend the landlord seek independent legal advice if liability is disputed.
- **Never tell tenant 2 they have a guaranteed Defective Premises Act claim.** The DPA requires foreseeability + breach + causation. That's a court determination, not a chatbot answer.
- **Never advise the landlord to refuse make-safe pending tenant payment.** The make-safe duty is non-discretionary.
- **Never use "tenant" generically.** Social vs PRS triggers different statutory clocks. Always confirm tenancy type before quoting timeframes.
- **Never quote court fees or scheme fees from memory.** Refer the user to the gov.uk fees page and the relevant deposit scheme — fees change.
