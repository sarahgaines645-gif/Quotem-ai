# Renters' Rights Act 2025

> **Source:** [legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/contents](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/contents)
> **Royal Assent:** 27 October 2025
> **Citation:** 2025 c. 26
> **Last verified:** 2026-04-26

## Naming — important

The Act's **official short title is "Renters' Rights Act 2025"** (not 2026). The Act was given Royal Assent on **27 October 2025**. Several internal Quotem docs and the `contract-writer.js` plugin currently refer to it as the "2026 Act" — that is **incorrect**; it is the 2025 Act, with substantive provisions commencing throughout 2026 and beyond via Statutory Instruments. Always cite as **"Renters' Rights Act 2025"** in any external document.

A key commencement date: **rules about private renting in England are changing on 1 May 2026** (per gov.uk eviction guidance, last verified 2026-04-26).

## Long title

> "An Act to make provision changing the law about rented homes, including provision abolishing fixed term assured tenancies and assured shorthold tenancies; imposing obligations on landlords and others in relation to rented homes and temporary and supported accommodation; and for connected purposes."

— legislation.gov.uk

## Structure of the Act

The Act has **5 Parts**, **149 sections**, and **6 Schedules**.

### Part 1 — Tenancy reform (Sections 1–62)

- **Chapter 1** — Assured tenancies (Sections 1–30)
- **Chapter 2** — Tenancies that cannot be assured tenancies (Sections 31–32)
- **Chapter 3** — Discrimination in rental market — England (Sections 33–42)
- **Chapter 4** — Discrimination in rental market — Wales (Sections 43–49)
- **Chapter 5** — Discrimination in rental market — Scotland (Sections 50–55)
- **Chapter 6** — Stating proposed rent and rental bidding (Sections 56–57)
- **Chapter 7** — Miscellaneous (Sections 58–62)

### Part 2 — Residential landlords (Sections 63–99)

- **Chapter 1** — Meaning of residential landlord (Section 63)
- **Chapter 2** — Landlord redress schemes (Sections 64–74)
- **Chapter 3** — Private Rented Sector Database (Sections 75–96)
- **Chapter 4** — Supplementary provision (Sections 97–99)

### Part 3 — Decent homes standard (Sections 100–101)

Extends the Decent Homes Standard to the private rented sector.

### Part 4 — Enforcement (Sections 102–136)

- **Chapter 1** — Sanctions (Sections 102–106)
- **Chapter 2** — Enforcement authorities (Sections 107–113)
- **Chapter 3** — Investigatory powers (Sections 114–136)

### Part 5 — General (Sections 137–149)

## Key changes — what landlords must know

> Source for all of this section: [legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/part/1](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/part/1) (verified 2026-04-26)

### Section 1 — All assured tenancies become periodic

Section 1 converts all assured tenancies to periodic. Fixed-term provisions are invalidated. Rent periods restricted to monthly or 28-day cycles. Quote from the Act: *"periodic tenancy under which the periods of the tenancy are the same as the rent periods."*

### Section 2 — Assured shorthold tenancies abolished

Removes Chapter 2 of Part 1 from the Housing Act 1988. The AST category is eliminated entirely.

### Section 3 — Possession grounds restructured

Schedule 2 of the Housing Act 1988 (grounds for possession) is substantially revised:

- New **section 8(4AA)** sets notice periods ranging from **2 weeks to 4 months** depending on the ground cited.
- New **section 7(5ZA)–(5E)** creates exceptions preventing possession orders on Grounds 1–5H or 6A in specific agricultural succession cases.
- **Ground 6B** becomes actionable (previously unavailable); courts may award compensation under new section 11A.

### Section 6 & 7 — Rent increase procedures

- Landlords must give **2 months' notice** of any rent increase.
- Tenants may apply directly to the First-tier Tribunal **within 6 months** of tenancy start.
- New sections 14ZA and 14ZB cap the new rent at the lower of (a) open-market rent or (b) the proposed amount.

### Section 8 — Rent payment timing

New **section 4B** prohibits rent in advance except for an initial pre-tenancy payment. Rent becomes due on "the first day of a rent period" if no compliant regular pattern is set.

### Section 21 of the 1988 Act — abolished

The Renters' Rights Act 2025 **abolishes Section 21 of the Housing Act 1988** (the "no-fault" eviction notice). After commencement, all possession claims must use Section 8 with a specified ground.

## Commencement timetable

> Source: [Renters' Rights Act 2025 (Commencement No. 1) Regulations 2025 — SI 2025/1354](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2025/1354/made)

- **27 December 2025** — first commencement: provisions facilitating Chapter 3 of Part 4 (investigatory powers).
- **1 May 2026** — main private renting reforms come into force (gov.uk eviction guidance).
- Further phased commencement via subsequent Statutory Instruments.

## Anti-fabrication rules for QB2 on this topic

- If asked about a **specific section number**, cite it as `Section X of the Renters' Rights Act 2025` and link to `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/section/X`.
- Do **not** invent ground numbers, notice periods, or commencement dates. If unsure, say "I don't have that fact verified — check legislation.gov.uk".
- Section 21 of the **Housing Act 1988** (no-fault eviction) is **NOT** the same as Section 21 of the **Renters' Rights Act 2025**. Don't conflate.
