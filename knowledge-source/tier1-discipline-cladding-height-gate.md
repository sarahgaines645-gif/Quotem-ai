# Discipline — Cladding Specification Is Gated by Building Height

> **Rule:** External-wall fire performance is gated by building height. There is no single national default for cladding material. Building height (and use class) must be established before any external-wall material is specified, installed or signed off.
> **Audience:** architects, specifiers, structural and building engineers, contractors, building-control officers, fire engineers, surveyors, landlords/freeholders commissioning works, Accountable Persons under the BSA 2022.
> **Last verified:** 2026-04-26

## The height bands

| Building height | What changes | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Below 11m** | Standard Approved Document B rules apply. No mandatory non-combustibility requirement on the external wall under Reg 7(2). | https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/fire-safety-approved-document-b |
| **11m – 18m** | Materials in the **external walls of relevant buildings** must achieve **European Class A2-s1, d0** or better (limited combustibility). "Relevant buildings" = blocks of flats, hospitals, residential care premises, dormitories in boarding schools, student accommodation, and (from 1 December 2022) hotels, hostels and boarding houses. | Building Regulations 2010, Reg 7(2) — https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2010/2214/regulation/7 |
| **Above 18m** | As above, **plus** the building is a **Higher-Risk Building (HRB)** under the Building Safety Act 2022 — gateway regime, Accountable Person duties, Building Safety Regulator oversight. New residential >18m granted approval after 30 September 2026 also requires a **second staircase**. | https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2022/30/contents/enacted |

## Definitions

### Class A2-s1, d0 (limited combustibility)

A reaction-to-fire classification under **BS EN 13501-1**:

- **A2** — limited combustibility (only A1 — non-combustible — is higher).
- **s1** — minimal smoke production.
- **d0** — no flaming droplets/particles.

Source: https://knowledge.bsigroup.com/products/fire-classification-of-construction-products-and-building-elements-classification-using-data-from-reaction-to-fire-tests

### Reg 7(2) — the cladding ban

The ban on combustible materials in external walls of relevant buildings was introduced in 2018 (originally 18m+) and updated in 2022. The 11m threshold has applied since **1 June 2022** for blocks of flats, hospitals, residential care premises, student accommodation and boarding-school dormitories. From **1 December 2022** the ban was extended to hotels, hostels and boarding houses.

Source (gov.uk overview): https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/banning-the-use-of-combustible-materials-in-the-external-walls-of-high-rise-residential-buildings
Source (2022 amending SI): https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2022/603/contents/made

### Higher-Risk Building (HRB)

Under the **Higher-Risk Buildings (Descriptions and Supplementary Provisions) Regulations 2023**, an HRB (in occupation) is a building that:

- Is at least **18m in height** OR has at least **7 storeys**, AND
- Contains **at least 2 residential units**.

Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2023/275/contents

### Accountable Person

Under the Building Safety Act 2022, every occupied HRB must have an **Accountable Person** (and a **Principal Accountable Person** if more than one) responsible for assessing and managing building-safety risks, registering the building with the Building Safety Regulator, and producing a Safety Case Report.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/accountable-persons-under-the-building-safety-act-2022

### Second-staircase rule

New residential buildings **above 18m** require a second staircase. The new guidance commences **30 September 2026**, with transitional arrangements for projects approved before that date and "sufficiently progressed" within 18 months (i.e. by 30 March 2028).
Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/news/calls-for-second-staircases-in-all-new-tall-residential-buildings

## The underlying rule, restated

Combustible cladding (timber rainscreen, ACM, HPL, EPS render carrier and similar) is prohibited in the external walls of a relevant building above 11m under Reg 7(2). A specifier writing such a material into a scope, a contractor installing it, a building-control officer signing it off, or a landlord/freeholder commissioning it on a relevant building above 11m, is non-compliant.

For any building above **18m**, the work additionally falls under the Building Safety Act gateway regime. Design and construction decisions on external-wall systems require competent fire-engineering input and Accountable Person sign-off, and pass through Gateway 2 (before construction) and Gateway 3 (before occupation).

## Diagnostic questions before specifying

If the brief mentions cladding, external wall, render system, EWI, balcony or rainscreen on a block of flats, care home, hospital, dormitory, student accommodation, hotel, hostel or boarding house, establish first:

1. **Building height** — top floor finished-floor level above lowest external ground.
2. **Number of storeys.**
3. **Use class** — residential / care / hospital / student / hotel/hostel.
4. **Project status** — new build, refurbishment, remediation. (Remediation work on existing non-compliant cladding has its own pathway under the Building Safety Act and the Cladding Safety Scheme.)

Without those answers, the material cannot be specified.

## Common errors and red flags

- A cladding spec produced before height and use class are confirmed.
- Combustible cladding (timber rainscreen, ACM, HPL, EPS render carrier) proposed on a relevant building above 11m.
- External-wall work on a building above 18m treated as a routine refurbishment scope rather than HRB gateway work.
- Reg 7(2) assumed to apply only above 18m — it has applied at 11m+ since 1 June 2022 for the original list of relevant buildings, and since 1 December 2022 for hotels, hostels and boarding houses.
- A reaction-to-fire claim that names "Class 0" (a withdrawn Approved Document B classification) instead of the BS EN 13501-1 Euroclass.
