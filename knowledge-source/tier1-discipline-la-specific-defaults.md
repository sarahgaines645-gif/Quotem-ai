# Discipline — Local-Authority-Specific Defaults: Establish Which LA First

> **Rule:** Some defaults are local-authority specific, not national. The relevant council must be identified before a recommendation is made on items where the convention is set by the LA, not by a BS/EN standard.
> **Audience:** specifiers, surveyors, contractors, planning consultants, council DLO/contracts teams, landlords (private and social), letting agents, legal/compliance reviewers.
> **Last verified:** 2026-04-26

## What varies by LA

| Item | Why it varies | First question |
|---|---|---|
| **Wheelie bin sizes / colours / lid coding** | LA waste contracts and recycling streams are negotiated locally. No national colour code. | "Which council? Are bins LA-supplied or property-owned?" |
| **Council DLO / framework repair schedules** | Each council's Direct Labour Organisation (or framework contractor) prices its own schedule of rates. National baselines do not bind those frameworks. | "Is this work going through a council schedule, or open-market?" |
| **Selective licensing schemes (PRS)** | Designated by individual LAs under Housing Act 2004 Part 3. Each scheme has its own conditions and fees. | "What's the postcode? Is there a selective licensing scheme there?" |
| **Additional HMO licensing** | LAs can extend HMO licensing beyond the mandatory threshold under Part 2 of the Housing Act 2004. Conditions and fees vary. | "Is the property in an additional HMO licensing area?" |
| **Planning policy** (extensions, changes of use, ASHP siting where Permitted Development is restricted) | Local Plan and Article 4 directions are LA-specific. | "Which LA's planning policy applies, and is there an Article 4 direction?" |

## What does NOT vary by LA

These are nationally consistent:

- **Building Regulations / Approved Documents** (B, F, L, M, K, etc.) — England-wide.
- **BS / BS EN product standards** — UK-wide.
- **Mandatory HMO threshold** — 5+ unrelated occupants forming 2+ households (Licensing of Houses in Multiple Occupation (Prescribed Description) (England) Order 2018). Same across England.
  Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2018/221/contents/made
- **Awaab's Law timeframes** (social rented sector, Phase 1 from 27 October 2025) — statutory and uniform across England.
- **Renters' Rights Act 2025** — uniform across England.
  Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/contents

## Wheelie bin standardisation (or lack of)

There is no national wheelie-bin colour or size standard. **Simpler Recycling** (the policy formerly known as "Consistent Collections") sets standardised waste *streams* across England (residual, dry recyclable mixed materials, food waste, garden waste) under sections 45A and 45AZA of the Environmental Protection Act 1990 (as amended by the Environment Act 2021), but bin colours, sizes and exact stream split remain a local decision.

- Defra guidance on Simpler Recycling: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/simpler-recycling-in-england-policy-update
- Environmental Protection Act 1990 (as amended): https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1990/43/contents

In practice, the council's website is the source of truth for bin colour, size and collection schedule for any given postcode.

## Selective licensing — finding the LA's scheme

A council may designate all or part of its area as a selective licensing area under **section 80, Housing Act 2004** if conditions are met (low housing demand, anti-social behaviour, poor property conditions, etc.). Each scheme has its own fees and conditions.

- Housing Act 2004, Part 3: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2004/34/part/3
- gov.uk landlord licensing overview: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/selective-licensing-in-the-private-rented-sector-a-guide-for-local-authorities
- Find a scheme by searching the council's "private rented sector" / "housing standards" pages.

## Additional HMO licensing

Beyond the **mandatory** scheme (5+ occupants forming 2+ households, sharing facilities), LAs can designate **additional HMO licensing** under **section 56, Housing Act 2004** to cover smaller HMOs in their area. Each scheme has its own fee and conditions.

- Housing Act 2004, Part 2: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2004/34/part/2
- gov.uk HMO licensing: https://www.gov.uk/house-in-multiple-occupation-licence

## Council repair schedules

Some councils publish their own schedule of rates for in-house works or framework contractors. Where public, these are useful cross-references but **do not replace** a national pricing baseline unless the contractor is on that council's framework. Procurement Act 2023 obligations apply to how those frameworks are designed and let.
Source (Procurement Act 2023): https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/54/contents

To find one: search `[council name] schedule of rates` or `[council name] DLO` on the council's procurement / housing pages.

## How to handle LA-specific questions

1. **Identify the LA** — postcode lookup on gov.uk gives the council name: https://www.gov.uk/find-local-council
2. **Visit the council's housing / waste / planning pages** for the specific convention.
3. **Apply the national default for the build itself**, but **flag** any LA-specific item (bin store sizing, licensing condition, planning constraint, Article 4 direction) as a check the landlord/contractor must confirm with the council before committing.

## In practice

For items with no national default, the right answer is not a price — it's a sourcing route:

> "There's no national default for this — it's set by the local authority. Which council is the property in? I'll point you to the right reference."

The product or works can still be costed at a national baseline; the **specification** comes from the LA.

## Common errors and red flags

- A "240L grey wheelie bin" assumed as a national default — colour and size depend on the LA.
- A national pricing baseline used against a council DLO or framework schedule when the contractor is not on that framework.
- "Is selective licensing in force?" answered without checking the specific LA — there are several hundred designated schemes across England.
- Permitted Development assumed without checking for an **Article 4 direction** in the LA.
- An HMO described as "definitely not licensable" without checking whether the LA operates an additional licensing scheme covering smaller HMOs.
