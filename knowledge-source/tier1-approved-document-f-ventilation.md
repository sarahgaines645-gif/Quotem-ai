## What Part F covers

Functional requirements from Schedule 1:

| Requirement | Subject |
|---|---|
| **F1** | Ventilation — provision of adequate means of ventilation |

The Document specifies how to provide that ventilation in practice — minimum airflow rates, types of system, prevention of moisture build-up, and indoor air quality.

## Four ventilation system types for dwellings (high level)

The 2021 edition recognises **four ventilation strategies** for dwellings (Volume 1, Section 1):

| System | What it is |
|---|---|
| **System 1** | Background ventilators + intermittent extract fans |
| **System 2** | Passive stack ventilation |
| **System 3** | Continuous mechanical extract ventilation (MEV) |
| **System 4** | Continuous mechanical supply and extract with heat recovery (MVHR) |

Each system has minimum airflow rates and design requirements. The exact rates per room (kitchen, bathroom, utility room) are tabulated in the PDF.

## Why this matters for landlords / surveyors

**Damp and mould** is overwhelmingly a ventilation problem in existing UK housing stock. The link between Part F (adequate ventilation) and:

- **Awaab's Law** (timeframes for tackling damp/mould in social housing — see `tier2-awaabs-law.md`)
- **HHSRS Hazard 1: Damp and mould growth** (see `tier2-hhsrs-pointer.md`)
- **The Decent Homes Standard** (now extended to PRS by RRA 2025 — see `tier1-renters-rights-act-2025.md`)

…means that any retrofit or repair quote dealing with mould, condensation, or window replacement should consider Part F implications.

## When Part F applies to existing buildings

- **Replacement windows** that include trickle vents — current Part F requires retention of background ventilation when windows are replaced (Building Regulations 2010, Schedule 1, F1(1)(b))
- **New extensions** — must have appropriate ventilation provision, and the existing dwelling's ventilation must not be compromised
- **Conversion of buildings** — full Part F applies as for new dwellings

## Practical advice QB2 can give

For damp/mould in an existing rented dwelling:
1. Check **trickle vent** provision (most pre-2006 windows have none, post-2006 may have but tenants commonly close them)
2. Check **bathroom and kitchen extract fans** — present, working, vented externally (not into a loft)
3. Check **passive stack vents** if originally fitted — still clear and unblocked
4. Check **occupancy density vs ventilation rate** — overcrowding can overwhelm a compliant system
5. Recommend a **heat-loss-aware retrofit** (insulation alone without ventilation upgrade typically makes mould worse)

For the Quotem product specifically, see the Awaab's Law file for the timeframes that apply if this is in a social tenancy.

## What QB2 should never do

- **Never specify a precise extract rate from memory** — refer to Approved Document F or the relevant British Standard.
- **Never claim mould is "just condensation"** when there's a building defect (e.g. cold bridge, broken DPC, leaking gutter) — that's an HHSRS Cat 1 hazard if it presents a significant risk.
- **Never propose blocking trickle vents** as a draught fix — it's a statutory ventilation provision.
