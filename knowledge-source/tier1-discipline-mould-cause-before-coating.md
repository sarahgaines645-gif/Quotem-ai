# Discipline — Fix the Cause of Mould Before Any Coating

> **Rule:** Anti-mould coating (fungicidal emulsion / additive) is applied AFTER the cause of the mould has been remediated — leak, ventilation deficit, cold bridge or construction defect. It is not a standalone treatment. In the social rented sector, painting over mould without investigating the cause is a documented Awaab's Law non-compliance pattern.
> **Audience:** specifiers, surveyors, contractors, environmental health officers, landlords (social and private), tenant advocates, legal/compliance reviewers.
> **Last verified:** 2026-04-26

## Statutory basis

- **Awaab's Law** — section 10A inserted into the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 by section 42 of the Social Housing (Regulation) Act 2023.
  Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/36/section/42
- **Hazards in Social Housing (Prescribed Requirements) (England) Regulations 2025** — set the prescribed timeframes for investigation and repair.
  Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2025/1080/contents/made
- **Phase 1 commenced 27 October 2025** — covers emergency hazards plus significant damp and mould. Phase 2 (2026) extends to excess cold/heat, falls, structural collapse, fire and electrical hazards. Phase 3 (2027) covers the remaining HHSRS hazards except overcrowding.
  Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/awaabs-law-guidance-for-social-landlords/awaabs-law-guidance-for-social-landlords-timeframes-for-repairs-in-the-social-rented-sector
- **HHSRS Hazard 1** — Damp and mould growth (Housing Act 2004, Part 1).
  Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/housing-health-and-safety-rating-system-guidance-for-landlords-and-property-related-professionals
- **Decent Homes Standard extension to PRS** — under the Renters' Rights Act 2025, Decent Homes will apply to private rented housing; HHSRS Cat 1 hazards (including damp and mould) become enforceable in the PRS.
  Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/contents

## Why painting over mould fails

Fungicidal coating kills surface spores but does not alter the moisture source. If the cause (condensation from inadequate ventilation, an active leak, a cold bridge) remains, mould returns within weeks. The statutory duty under Awaab's Law is to **investigate**, **make safe**, and **complete preventative work** within prescribed timeframes — cosmetic disguise satisfies none of those steps. In a private tenancy, the same hazard sits under HHSRS / fitness for human habitation duties under the Homes (Fitness for Human Habitation) Act 2018.
Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2018/19/contents/enacted

## Diagnostic order

Work through these checks in order. Stop at the first that explains the mould; otherwise continue.

1. **Active leak** — roof, plumbing, overflow, gutter, balcony detail. Fix and dry the substrate before any other work. BRE publishes general guidance on dampness investigation: https://www.bregroup.com/insights/dampness-in-buildings
2. **Cold spot / thermal bridge** — uninsulated lintel, single-glazed reveal, missing cavity-wall insulation. Surface temperature falls below dew point and condensation forms locally. Approved Document L sets fabric performance requirements: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/conservation-of-fuel-and-power-approved-document-l
3. **Ventilation deficit** — bathroom or kitchen extract not working, blocked or not vented to outside; trickle vents missing. Tested against Approved Document F minimums (see below).
4. **Occupant moisture load** — drying laundry indoors, no extract use, gas hob without ducted extract. Note: under Awaab's Law and the Housing Ombudsman's spotlight reports, "tenant lifestyle" alone is not an acceptable conclusion — the landlord must have provided working ventilation and adequate fabric performance first.
   Source (Housing Ombudsman): https://www.housing-ombudsman.org.uk/reports/spotlight-reports/spotlight-on-damp-and-mould/
5. **Construction defect** — rising damp (rare, often misdiagnosed; verify with a calcium-carbide / gravimetric test before treatment), penetrating damp through defective render/pointing, DPC bridging.
6. **Then — and only then** — fungicidal wash, mist coat, fungicidal emulsion or additive top coats.

## Approved Document F minimums (the ventilation baseline, dwellings)

- **Intermittent bathroom extract:** 15 l/s in any room with a bath or shower.
- **Continuous mechanical extract:** 8 l/s minimum (kitchen 13 l/s).
- **Kitchen hob hood:** 30 l/s if over the hob, 60 l/s elsewhere — **ducted to outside**. Recirculating hoods do not satisfy ADF.
- Extract must vent **outside** — never into a loft or cavity.

Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ventilation-approved-document-f

## Awaab's Law clock for damp and mould (Phase 1)

For a significant damp/mould hazard, from the date the social landlord becomes aware:

| Step | Timeframe |
|---|---|
| Begin investigation | within **10 working days** |
| Provide written summary to tenant | within **3 working days** of investigation concluding |
| Complete safety work | within **5 working days** after investigation concludes |
| Begin preventative work | within **5 working days** of investigation concluding (or within **12 weeks** if practical preparation needed) |

For an emergency hazard (immediate risk to health and safety), investigation and any necessary safety work must be undertaken **as soon as reasonably practicable, within 24 hours**.

Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/awaabs-law-guidance-for-social-landlords/awaabs-law-guidance-for-social-landlords-timeframes-for-repairs-in-the-social-rented-sector

## In practice

A "treat the mould" instruction or scope is rarely just a paint line. A compliant remediation typically includes:

1. **Investigation** — surveyor visit, moisture readings, photo evidence, written summary to the tenant.
2. **Cause fix** — could be any of: gutter clear, leak repair, extract fan renewal, trickle vents fitted, insulation top-up at cold spot, render repair, DPC works.
3. **Fungicidal wash** — to the affected surface.
4. **Redecoration** — mist coat plus two coats of fungicidal emulsion (or standard emulsion with a fungicidal additive).

Specifying only the redecoration line for a damp/mould job under-scopes the work and, in the social rented sector, walks the landlord into a statutory non-compliance.

## Common errors and red flags

- Anti-mould coating quoted as a standalone fix with no investigation line.
- "We'll just paint over it" responses from a contractor or housing officer to a tenant report — under Awaab's Law and the Housing Ombudsman's spotlight findings, this is a recognised failure pattern.
- Investigation line dropped from a damp/mould scope of works because it "feels like overkill" — investigation is the duty, not a discretionary extra.
- Rising-damp diagnoses without a moisture-content test (calcium-carbide / gravimetric) — most cases assumed to be rising damp on visual inspection are penetrating damp, condensation or bridged DPC.
- Ducted extract terminating in a loft or cavity rather than outside.
- For social-housing damp/mould enquiries, missing or late acknowledgement of the **10 working days** investigation clock.
